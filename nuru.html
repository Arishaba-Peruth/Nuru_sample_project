Nuru Yangu Community-Based Organisation (CBO)

Empowering Lives. Restoring Hope.

"Nuru Yangu," Swahili for My Light, symbolizes our goal: to illuminate lives through opportunity, dignity, and resilience.

Learn More About Us

About Us

Founded in 2025 in Homa Bay County, Nuru Yangu CBO is a grassroots organization committed to empowering vulnerable groups, particularly widows, the elderly, and the youth, through targeted, sustainable community-driven initiatives.

We exist to address systemic challenges in our communities such as poverty, youth unemployment, drug and substance abuse, food insecurity, gender vulnerability, limited access to healthcare and education, environmental degradation, and the growing impact of climate change.

Our Vision:

To build a resilient, empowered, and thriving community that upholds dignity, inclusivity, and sustainable development and economic independence.

Our Mission:

To empower vulnerable groups through skills development, sustainable economic initiatives, social awareness, and capacity building while promoting education, health, environmental conservation, and food security.

Our Strategic Approach

We use a participatory, community-led model driven by collaboration with local leaders, government agencies, schools, faith-based groups, and civil society organizations. Sustainability, inclusion, and accountability are our guiding principles.

Our Thematic Areas

Economic Empowerment

We support income-generating activities and financial literacy for widows, youth, and the elderly to improve livelihoods.

  • Vocational training (tailoring, agribusiness, crafts)
  • Formation and support of self-help groups
  • Access to microloans and savings models

Youth Development & Drug Abuse Prevention

With increasing cases of drug abuse among idle youth, we focus on prevention, awareness, and mentorship.

  • Community sensitization and peer-led campaigns
  • School and church outreach programs
  • Linkages to rehabilitation and support services

Education Empowerment

We promote access to education as a right and tool for transformation.

  • Support for orphans and vulnerable children (fees, materials)
  • Life skills and mentorship programs
  • Holiday camps and literacy outreach

Health and Mental Wellness

We prioritize accessible, preventive healthcare and mental well-being for all, especially elders and adolescent girls.

  • Community health outreaches and checkups
  • Menstrual hygiene campaigns
  • Mental health awareness and counseling referrals

Climate Action & Environmental Protection

We engage communities in environmental conservation and promote climate-smart practices.

  • Tree planting and clean-up drives
  • Community-led land restoration activities and soil conservation practices
  • Climate-smart farming training
  • Youth-led environmental clubs in schools

Food Security & Nutrition

We promote sustainable farming practices and access to nutritious food.

  • Community kitchen gardens for widows/elderly
  • School feeding programs
  • Nutrition education for families

Our Impact: Projections (2025–2030)

Short-Term (2025–2026)

  • 500 youth with anti-drug campaigns
  • 3 active self-help groups
  • 50 vulnerable learners
  • 5,000 trees planted

Medium-Term (2027–2028)

  • Establish a Community Empowerment Centre
  • Train 500 youth in green job skills
  • Launch a widow-led revolving fund

Long-Term (By 2030)

  • Scale projects to neighboring sub-counties
  • Become a model CBO for grassroots empowerment
  • Secure long-term partnerships with donors and agencies

About the Founder

Dr. Calvine Otieno Odiwuor

Dr. Calvine Otieno Odiwuor is a Kenyan, born and bred in Homabay County. He is an academic researcher and educator whose expertise spans actuarial science, mathematics, statistics, and computational analysis.

Currently serving as an Assistant Lecturer at Tom Mboya University, Dr. Calvine is deeply committed to community transformation through knowledge, empowerment, and innovation.

His passion for a more equitable and sustainable future for all lies at the heart of Nuru Yangu's vision—where practical solutions, education, and local action illuminate the path to lasting change.
